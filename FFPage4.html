On April 12, 2011, Wasting Light, the band's seventh album, was released. Dave Grohl and the band recorded the album in Dave's garage and went on a garage tour, playing at fan's home garages. On November 10, 2014, Sonic Highways was released. An HBO documentary was released alongside it, showcasing the recording process. On September 15, 2017, Concrete & Gold was released. For this album, they enlisted Greg Kurstin, a music producer who had never produced a rock album before (only pop).

Wasting Light

In 2011, the Foo Fighters released their seventh studio album. It was recorded not only in Dave Grohl's garage but also to analog tape. No digital recording or ProTools was used during the making of the album and tape had to be spliced and cut to edit the music - the old-school way!

Sonic Highways

In 2014, the Foo Fighters traveled across the United States to famous cities with famous music history. Eight songs were recorded in different cities across the U.S. in famous music studios. A documentary aired on HBO showing the recording process as well.



Concrete & Gold

In 2017, the ninth Foo Fighters album was released. The band hired Greg Kurstin to produce the album, a producer that had only worked on pop albums up to that point. This was the first album to feature keyboardist Rami Jaffee as a permanent member.

On November 2, 1999, There Is Nothing Left To Lose was released. It saw the band as a 3-piece - Dave Grohl, Nate Mendel, and Taylor Hawkins. On October 22, 2002, One By One was released and saw the addition of lead guitarist Chris Shiflett to the band.

There Is Nothing Left To Lose

In 1999, the third Foo Fighters' album was released. Dave Grohl made the decision to record the album in his home studio which led to a more laid-back and alternative sound. Dave Grohl has gone on record and said that this might be the favorite Foo Fighters album they've made.



One By One

In 2002, the Foo Fighters gained Chris Shiflett on lead guitar after holding auditions. Returning to the studio, the band had a difficult time with the initial sessions of the album. After restarting, they eventually ended up with the finished product. This album had a darker, more rock-driven direction.
